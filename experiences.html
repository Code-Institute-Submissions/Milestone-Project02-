Fine Dining

Embark on a culinary tour of the world at The St. Regis Bora Bora Resort. With four outstanding restaurants and a cocktail bar by the beach, our resort serves a wealth of culinary delights from modernized French Polynesian fare to authentic Italian dishes. All our upscale offerings are crafted from fresh, island-sourced ingredients to honor the bounty of Bora Bora's natural landscape, as you dine in our breathtaking locales.

Bam Boo Restaurant

Bam Boo revitalises the Resort’s Asian dining experience with the introduction of wok and teppanyaki options alongside our guests’ longtime sushi favourites. Traditional Chinese wok dishes such as Kung Pao chicken and Sichuan style shrimp star on the opening menu while local lagoon fish is accomodate in a chinese style. On the Japanese menu, the Sushi Chef sublimate the freshest caught fish with delicate flair. Sushi bestsellers include the Volcano and the Red Dragon Roll. Variety is further enhanced with the addition of a Teppanyaki stove, visible from the counter seating, from which guests may order hot dishes.

The Bora Bora VIP Bloody Mary

In 1934, famed barman Fernand Petiot perfected the recipe for a vodka-and-tomato juice cocktail, known as the Bloody Mary at The St. Regis New York’s King Cole Bar. Originally christened the “Bloody Mary”, the cocktail was renamed the “Red Snapper” so as not to offend the hotel’s refined clientele. After more than 80 years, the iconic Bloody Mary remains the signature cocktail of the St. Regis.

Lagoon Restaurant by Jean-Georges is The St. Regis Bora Bora Resort’s exclusive signature restaurant. Bora-Bora Pearl Beach Resort & Spa

Tours

Bora Bora VIP Resort's offers a variety of exciting activities for your pleasure in paradise. With three pristine beaches, two swimming pools and a lovely Lagoonarium filled with tropical fish and other sea creatures, our hotel on the water is an aquatic playground. Complimentary watersports at the Main Beach include stand-up paddle boarding and kayaking. Relax at Miri Miri Spa by Clarins, work out in our fitness center or play a set on our tennis court. For offsite adventure, you'll find scuba, snorkeling, reef fishing, deep-sea fishing and a traditional Polynesian Dinner Show.

Priavte snorkel adventure and motu lunch

eet your local guide at the Resort dock and head across Bora Bora's famous lagoon on a motorized outrigger canoe, paying homage to the traditional seafaring vessel of the Polynesians. Your private tour takes you to three incredible locations for underwater adventure: the Coral Garden, where you'll see colorful reef fish amid dreamlike coral structures; the shallow waters, where you'll interact with mesmerizing stingrays; and the Pacific Ocean, where schools of blacktip reef sharks and lemon sharks roam. Finish your day with a fresh lunch on your guide's private motu (island), where you'll enjoy a taste of authentic Polynesia.

The Lagoonarium

The Resort's private Lagoonarium is a protected underwater sanctuary for myriad species of tropical fish and coral, including the remarkable Napoleon fish. Several pontoons allow easy access for snorkeling at leisure. The visit of a local biologist at the hotel allow customers to be informed about the marine life. Fish and Sharks feeding three times a week.
